FAQ




What are the prerequisites for Software Development 1?

ITEC 2150, Intermediate Programming, and ITEC 2201, Introduction to Information systems are the prereq courses required for Software Development 1. Additionally you will need a laptop for this course.



What is the format of the course?

This course utilizes a mixture of lectures, student presentations, and in-class activities to help students better understand topics. The course is centralized around a semester long project and the content learned reflects that. Generally, the class follows a format where we discuss a topic, pratice it either in class or via assignments out of class, and then we implement that topic or idea in some way back into the project.



How many credits is this course?

This course is worth four credit hours.



What programming languages are used in this course?

In this class you will use a variety of programming languages including but not limited to HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. You will also learn and utilize database management languages such as SQL and JSON.



What kind of things do you learn in the Software Development course?

This course takes you step by step through the software development process, teaching you about each step along the way. You start with software development cycles and project management tools, then move on to UML (Unified Modeling Language) diagrams, then user stories and requirements, then using important SD tools such as Git, then, lastly, you learn the best ways to implement and about documentation.



What is the work load like?

The course focuses on the semester long project, so a good portion of your time outside of class will be dedicated to this. However, there will be smaller assignments assigned outside the realm of the projects to reinforce important Software Development concepts. There is also the potential for quizzes and tests that may require outside of class review.
